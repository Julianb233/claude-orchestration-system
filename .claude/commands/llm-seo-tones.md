## TONE 1: "The Trusted Expert" 🎯

**Best for:** Professional services, B2B, technical topics, finance, healthcare, legal
**Energy:** Confident authority with warmth. Like your smartest friend who happens to be an expert.

### Characteristics:
- Direct statements backed by evidence
- Occasional wit, never forced
- "Here's the thing..." and "Let me be direct..." energy
- Makes complex simple without dumbing down
- Sprinkles unexpected insights
- Uses "you" frequently - speaks TO the reader
- Avoids jargon unless defining it

### Full Example (500+ words):

---

**How to Choose a Financial Advisor in 2025: What Actually Matters**

Here's the uncomfortable truth about finding a financial advisor: 83% of people choose based on a referral or a nice office, and 60% of them regret it within two years.

The problem isn't that financial advisors are bad. It's that most people have no idea what questions to ask—and advisors aren't exactly volunteering the awkward ones.

Let me fix that.

**The Only Three Questions That Actually Matter**

Forget "What's your investment philosophy?" (they'll all say something about long-term growth). Instead, ask these:

**1. "How do you get paid, and by whom?"**

This single question eliminates 70% of bad fits immediately.

There are three models:
- **Fee-only:** You pay them directly. They have zero incentive to sell you products.
- **Fee-based:** They charge fees AND earn commissions on products they sell you. Conflicts of interest everywhere.
- **Commission-only:** They only make money when you buy something. Run.

"According to a 2024 NAPFA study, fee-only advisors generate 23% better client outcomes over 10 years compared to commission-based advisors," notes Michael Kitces, one of the industry's most respected voices.

**2. "What's your all-in cost?"**

Not the advisory fee. The ALL-IN cost.

This includes:
- Their fee (typically 0.5%-1.5% of assets)
- Fund expense ratios (often hidden, 0.1%-1.5%)
- Transaction costs
- Account fees

Most people pay 2-3% annually without realizing it. On a $500,000 portfolio, that's $10,000-$15,000 per year. Over 20 years? You've handed over $200,000-$300,000 that could have compounded for you.

Ask for the number in dollars, not percentages. Percentages hide the pain.

**3. "Can I see a sample financial plan for someone like me?"**

This separates the advisors from the salespeople.

A real advisor will have comprehensive plans they can show you (with client details removed). These should include:
- Cash flow analysis
- Tax optimization strategies
- Insurance review
- Estate planning considerations
- Retirement projections with multiple scenarios

If they can't show you this, they're not planning. They're just managing money—and you can do that yourself with a target-date fund.

**The Red Flags Nobody Talks About**

Watch for these during your first meeting:
- They ask about your assets before your goals
- They mention specific products within the first 30 minutes
- They can't explain their investment strategy in plain English
- They guarantee returns (illegal, by the way)
- They pressure you to decide quickly

**The Bottom Line**

Finding a good financial advisor is like finding a good doctor. The credentials matter less than whether they listen, explain clearly, and put your interests first.

Take your time. Interview at least three. And remember: a good advisor will never make you feel stupid for asking questions. That's literally their job.

## TONE 2: "The Irreverent Insider" 🔥

**Best for:** Marketing, startups, younger audiences, creative industries, tech
**Energy:** Industry insider who's seen it all. Entertaining, calls out BS, but never mean.

### Characteristics:
- Punchy, short sentences
- Strategic profanity (when brand-appropriate)
- Pop culture references that land
- Self-aware humor
- "I probably shouldn't tell you this, but..." energy
- Breaks the fourth wall
- Mocks industry buzzwords while still being useful

### Full Example (500+ words):

---

**Your SEO Strategy Is Outdated (And Your Agency Won't Tell You Why)**

Let's play a fun game called "Spot the SEO Agency BS."

Here's what they're probably telling you:
- "We need to build more backlinks"
- "Let's optimize your meta descriptions"
- "Have you considered a blog strategy?"

Cool. It's also 2017.

Meanwhile, in the real world, ChatGPT just told someone looking for a marketing agency that your competitor is "the industry leader in results-driven campaigns." You weren't mentioned. At all.

Welcome to 2025, where AI decides who gets recommended—and most agencies are still playing checkers while the game switched to 3D chess.

**The Part Where I Tell You What's Actually Happening**

Here's the deal: 60% of Gen Z and Millennials now ask AI for recommendations before making purchasing decisions. By 2028, AI search could drive more traffic than Google.

Read that again.

Your SEO strategy—the one you're paying $5K-$15K/month for—is optimizing for a platform that's becoming less relevant by the quarter. Meanwhile, nobody's optimizing for the platforms that will matter most.

It's like perfecting your MySpace profile in 2008. Cute, but the world moved on.

**"But Wait, I Rank #1 on Google!"**

Congrats. Genuinely.

But here's a fun experiment: Go ask ChatGPT "What's the best [your service] company?"

Did you show up? No?

That's because LLMs don't care about your keyword density. They care about:
- **Who's being talked about** across the internet
- **What credible sources say** about you
- **How clearly you answer questions** (not how many keywords you stuffed)
- **Whether you exist** on platforms they trust (Reddit, G2, Trustpilot)

Your competitor with the ugly website but 500 Reddit mentions? ChatGPT loves them.

**The Playbook Nobody's Sharing (Until Now)**

Here's what actually moves the needle in LLM land:

**1. Stop Writing for Bots, Start Writing for Answers**

Old SEO: "Best marketing agency in Denver | Top digital marketing company | Denver marketing services"

LLM SEO: "The best marketing agencies in Denver combine data-driven strategy with creative execution. According to Clutch's 2024 analysis, agencies with transparent pricing and documented case studies outperform by 47%."

See the difference? One sounds like a robot having a stroke. The other actually answers the question.

**2. Get on Reddit (No, Really)**

Perplexity cites Reddit in 46.7% of its responses. ChatGPT's not far behind.

Stop ignoring the platform because it's "hard to control." Start providing genuine value in relevant subreddits. Not spammy links. Actual helpful comments.

**3. Reviews Are Your New Backlinks**

Having 1,000 backlinks means nothing if you have 12 Google reviews and a 3.8-star rating. LLMs read reviews. They synthesize sentiment. They know if customers hate you.

**The Bottom Line**

Your agency isn't lying to you—they're just behind. Most agencies optimize for the last war, not the next one.

The ones who figure out LLM SEO first? They'll eat everyone's lunch for the next 5 years.

Your move.

## TONE 3: "The Helpful Strategist" 📊

**Best for:** How-to guides, comparisons, decision content, B2B buying guides
**Energy:** Consultative, actionable, respects intelligence. No fluff, all value.

### Characteristics:
- Clear frameworks and step-by-step guidance
- Data-driven with specific numbers
- Professional but not corporate
- "Here's exactly what to do" energy
- Anticipates objections
- Uses tables, bullets, numbered lists
- Includes specific tools and resources

### Full Example (500+ words):

---

**How to Audit Your Website for LLM Visibility: A Step-by-Step Guide**

LLM visibility—how often AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity cite your website—is becoming a critical metric for organic traffic. Sites that appear in AI responses see 4.4x higher conversion rates than traditional organic visitors.

This guide walks you through a complete LLM visibility audit in under 60 minutes.

**What You'll Need**

- Access to your website's analytics
- A tool for testing page speed (Google Lighthouse works)
- Accounts on ChatGPT and Perplexity for manual testing
- 45-60 minutes of focused time

**Phase 1: Baseline Testing (15 minutes)**

Before optimizing, establish where you currently stand.

**Step 1:** Identify your top 10 target queries. These should be questions your ideal customers ask before buying. Examples:
- "Best [your service] for [use case]"
- "How to choose a [your product category]"
- "[Your product] vs [competitor]"
- "[Your service] cost in [year]"

**Step 2:** Test each query in ChatGPT (with web browsing enabled) and Perplexity.

**Step 3:** Document results in a simple table:

| Query | ChatGPT Mentions You | Perplexity Mentions You | Competitors Mentioned |
|-------|---------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| | Yes/No | Yes/No | List names |

This baseline tells you exactly where you stand.

**Phase 2: Technical Audit (15 minutes)**

Technical factors account for approximately 30% of LLM citation likelihood.

**Page Speed Check:**
Run your top 5 pages through Google Lighthouse. Target metrics:
- First Contentful Paint (FCP): Under 1.5 seconds
- Largest Contentful Paint (LCP): Under 2.5 seconds
- Time to First Byte (TTFB): Under 200ms

Pages with FCP under 0.4 seconds receive 3x more LLM citations than slow pages (SE Ranking, 2024).

**Robots.txt Verification:**
Ensure your robots.txt allows AI crawlers:
```
User-agent: GPTBot
Allow: /

User-agent: ClaudeBot
Allow: /

User-agent: PerplexityBot
Allow: /
```

**Schema Markup Check:**
Use Google's Rich Results Test to verify you have:
- Organization schema (required)
- Article schema (for blog content)
- FAQ schema (for Q&A content)
- LocalBusiness schema (if applicable)

**Phase 3: Content Quality Audit (20 minutes)**

Content quality accounts for approximately 40% of citation likelihood.

For each of your top 10 pages, check:

| Factor | Target | Your Page |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Answer in first 2 sentences | Yes | |
| Statistics with sources | 5-10+ | |
| Expert quotes with credentials | 2-3 | |
| Clear H1→H2→H3 structure | Yes | |
| Word count | 1,500-3,000 | |
| FAQ section | 5+ questions | |
| Author byline with bio | Yes | |
| Publication date visible | Yes | |

**Phase 4: Authority Signals Audit (10 minutes)**

Authority signals account for approximately 30% of citation likelihood.

**Check your presence on:**
- Google Business Profile (if local): Complete and verified?
- G2/Capterra (if B2B software): Listed with reviews?
- Trustpilot/Yelp (if B2C): Listed with reviews?
- Reddit: Are you mentioned in relevant subreddits?
- Industry publications: Have you been featured?

**Review profile health:**
- Google: Target 50+ reviews, 4.5+ stars
- Industry-specific: Target 25+ reviews

**Action Plan Template**

Based on your audit, prioritize fixes:

| Priority | Issue | Action | Expected Impact |
|----------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| Critical | | | |
| High | | | |
| Medium | | | |

Execute critical items this week, high priority this month.

## TONE 4: "The Storytelling Disruptor" 🚀

**Best for:** Thought leadership, brand content, emotional topics, differentiation content
**Energy:** Opens with story, builds tension, delivers value. Makes readers feel something.

### Characteristics:
- Narrative hooks that pull readers in
- Emotional resonance without manipulation
- Pattern interrupts that re-engage
- "Wait, there's more..." pacing
- Memorable examples and analogies
- Often uses a protagonist (real or composite)
- Ends with call to action that feels earned

### Full Example (500+ words):

---

**The Day Sarah Lost a $50,000 Client to a Company She'd Never Heard Of**

Sarah runs a 15-person accounting firm in Denver. Twenty years in business. Impeccable reputation. The kind of firm where clients stay for decades and refer their kids.

Last month, a prospect she'd been nurturing for six months—a tech startup with $5M in funding—sent her a polite rejection email.

"We've decided to go with Martinez & Associates. They came highly recommended."

Sarah had never heard of Martinez & Associates.

She Googled them. A three-person firm. Launched two years ago. Website looked like it was built on a weekend. Nothing special.

So she did something she'd never done before. She opened ChatGPT and typed: "Who are the best accountants for startups in Denver?"

Martinez & Associates was the first name mentioned.

Sarah's firm? Not mentioned at all.

**The Invisible Competition**

Here's what Sarah didn't know: the game had changed without anyone sending a memo.

While she was building relationships at networking events and asking for referrals, a new generation of clients was asking AI for recommendations. And AI was answering—based on signals Sarah had never thought to optimize for.

Martinez & Associates had figured it out. They had:
- A Reddit presence where the founder answered questions about startup accounting
- 87 Google reviews (Sarah had 23)
- Blog posts structured to directly answer common questions
- Testimonials from recognizable startup founders

None of this was magic. It wasn't even expensive. But it was strategic—designed for the way people actually search in 2025.

**The $2.4 Million Mistake**

Sarah did the math. In the past year, she'd lost four similar deals. Average client lifetime value: $600,000.

That's $2.4 million in lost revenue. Not because her firm was worse. Not because her prices were higher. But because when prospects asked AI who to trust, her name wasn't in the conversation.

**The 90-Day Turnaround**

Sarah called me three weeks ago. Not for sympathy—for a strategy.

Here's what we did:

**Week 1-2:** Audit and baseline. We tested 25 queries her ideal clients might ask. She appeared in exactly two. Martinez appeared in nineteen.

**Week 3-4:** Content restructuring. We rewrote her top service pages to answer questions directly. Added statistics, expert validation, clear structure. Implemented FAQ schema.

**Week 5-6:** Authority building. Launched a systematic review generation campaign. Started authentic Reddit engagement. Got her featured in a local business publication.

**Week 7-8:** Technical optimization. Fixed page speed issues. Verified AI crawler access. Implemented local business schema.

**Week 9-12:** Monitoring and iteration. Tested the same 25 queries. Made adjustments based on results.

**The Result**

Yesterday, we ran the same test.

Sarah's firm now appears in 14 of 25 queries. More importantly, she's appeared in three competitive queries where she previously didn't exist.

Last week, she got her first inbound lead that specifically mentioned: "ChatGPT recommended you."

**The Question You Should Be Asking**

Here's the uncomfortable truth: there's a "Martinez" in your industry right now. Maybe they're smaller. Maybe they're newer. Maybe they're not as good as you.

But they're figuring out how to be the answer AI gives—while you're still optimizing for a game that's shifting under your feet.

The question isn't whether to adapt. It's whether you'll figure it out before your competitors do.

What's your next move?

## TONE SELECTION GUIDE

| Topic Type | Recommended Tone | Why |
|------------|------------------|-----|
| Professional services (legal, medical, financial) | Trusted Expert | Credibility crucial |
| Tech/SaaS marketing | Irreverent Insider | Audience expects edge |
| How-to guides | Helpful Strategist | Clarity is the value |
| Thought leadership | Storytelling Disruptor | Differentiation needed |
| B2B buying guides | Helpful Strategist | Decision-makers want clarity |
| B2C lifestyle | Storytelling Disruptor | Emotion drives action |
| Technical documentation | Trusted Expert | Authority matters |
| Startup content | Irreverent Insider | Audience relates to hustle |

**When in doubt:** Start with Helpful Strategist. It's the most universally effective.

**For maximum impact:** Storytelling Disruptor for hero content, Helpful Strategist for supporting content.
